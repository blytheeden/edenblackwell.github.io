Recent Research and Projects

My preliminary work on impacts of climate change on smallholder farmers in Ethiopia was accepted for a poster session American Anthropological Association's Annual Meeting in Seattle. It was a great opportunity to connect with other anthropologists in the country as well as see the work of others in other regions on the continent.
Since I try to keep one foot in the the health policy world while working on my doctorate, I'm always delighted to talk to students of health policy or public health. In November, and for the third year now, I gave a lecture about recent health policy and related research in Mississippi to master's students in health administration at the University of Southern Miss. My slides are available here.
Just back from a few weeks of fieldwork on the outskirts of a Hawassa City in Ethiopia where I supported research related to stress and child development. While there I had the opportunity to do exploratory work related to my interests in climate change and health. I led focus groups and conducted individual interviews with farmers about their perceptions of change in long-term weather patterns and how they were coping with the impacts to their livelihoods and the health of their families.
